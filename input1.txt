The Renaissance was a profound cultural movement that began in Italy during the 14th century and spread across Europe. It marked a period of renewed interest in art, science, and the classical knowledge of Ancient Greece and Rome.

This era saw the emergence of remarkable artists such as Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo, whose works remain influential to this day. The Renaissance also fostered advancements in science, with figures like Nicolaus Copernicus challenging the long-held views of the cosmos.

Politically, the period was characterized by the rise of powerful city-states and the decline of feudal structures. Economically, it was a time of increased trade and urbanization, laying the groundwork for the modern age.
